Description

A member of the Bollyjee club, Jelix, likes to share foods. One of Jelix's favourite food is Snacks. One day when he was training in a training room, he brought snacks for him to share. Initially, there are $$$N$$$ that are brought to the training room. Then, Jelix and $$$K$$$ of his friends will receive snacks.

Jelix is a kind person. So, he doesn't want to play favour (for example one must not receive snacks more than others) so everybody will get the same amount of snacks. However, he does not like bringing home extra snacks (formally, the snacks must be divided out and there must not be any remainder). Your job is to help Jelix decide whether or not the snacks that he brought can be divided according to those two rules.

Input Format

Input starts with a positive integer denoting the number of cases. Each case consists of two positive integers $$$N$$$ ($$$1 \le N \le 100000$$$)and $$$K$$$ ($$$1 \le K < N \le 100000$$$) as explained from the description.

Output Format

For every case, output Kasus #X: S where $$$X$$$ denotes the case number and $$$S$$$ is a string ya if it is possible to divide the snacks according to the rules above or tidak if Jelix can not divide the snacks according to the rules above.

Sample Input

3
6 2
56 7
21 4

Sample Output 1

Kasus #1: ya
Kasus #2: ya
Kasus #3: tidak
