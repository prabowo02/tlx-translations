Description

Mr. Chanek is a well known scientist in the Nation of Chanek. Mr. Chanek is researching about monsters which often attack the Nation of Chanek.

Mr. Chanek has $N$ experimental monsters lining from left to right and numbered from $1$ to $N$. The $i$-th monster has power $A_i$. Mr. Chanek would like to do $M$ mergings. Mr. Chanek can choose a monster and merge it with the monster directly to the right of the former monster. After the merging, the two monsters will transform into a new monster with power equivalent to the sum of the powers of the two merged monsters.

Now Mr. Chanek is wondering, after $M$ mergings, what is the maximum monster's power he has.

Input Format

The first line consists of two integers $N$ and $M$, representing the number of experimental monsters Mr. Chanek has and the number of mergings to be done, respectively.

The next line consists of $N$ integers $A_i$ representing the power of the $i$-th experimental monster.

Output Format

An integer representing the maximum experimental monster power after $M$ mergings.

Sample Input 

7 3
1 5 2 5 3 4 1

Sample Output 

15

Explanation

First merging, merge the monsters at the third and the fourth positions. The monster configuration will then be 1 5 7 3 4 1.

Second merging, merge the monsters at the second and the third positions. The monster configuration will then be 1 12 3 4 1.

Third merging, merge the monsters at the second and the third positions. The monster configuration will then be 1 15 4 1.

The maximum monster power after three mergings is $15$. One can prove that $15$ is the maximum power that can be obtained.

Constraints

For all subtasks

  • $2 \le N \le 100\,000$
  • $1 \le M \le N-1$
  • $1 \le A_i \le 100\,000$ for $1 \le i \le N$

Subtask 1 (18 points)

  • $1 \le N \le 8$

Subtask 2 (29 points)

  • $1 \le N \le 1\,000$

Subtask 3 (14 points)

  • All values of $A_i$ are equal for $1 \le i \le N$

Subtask 3 (39 points)

  • No additional constaints.
