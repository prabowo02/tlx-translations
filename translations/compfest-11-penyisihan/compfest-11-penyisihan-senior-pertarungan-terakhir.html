Description

Mr. Chanek is playing his favorite video game. In the game, he has $N$ heroes standing in order from the first hero on the left to the $N$-th hero on the right. Hero $i$ has a power level of $A_i$.

Mr. Chanek is currently facing the final boss. Mr. Chanek's best friend, Mr. Dengklek, gave him a tip that to defeat the final boss, he must divide his heroes to exactly $M$ platoons by placing $M - 1$ barriers between two heroes. There must be at most one barrier between two adjacent heroes. The heroes which are not divided from the barriers form a platoon. The platoons are numbered from 1 to $M$ from left to right. The platoons must be divided in such a way that the power level of the strongest hero in the $i$-th platoon is exactly $B_i$.

Now Mr. Chanek is curious, how many possible configurations of platoons are there such that he can defeat the final boss.

Input Format

The first line contains 2 positive integers $N$ and $M$, the number of heroes and the number of platoons.

The next line contains $N$ integers $A_i$, the power level of the $i$-th hero.

The next line contains $M$ integers $B_i$, the maximum power level of the $i$-th platoon.

Output Format

An integer representing the number of platoon configurations. As the number can be large, output the answer in modulo $1\,000\,000\,007$.

Sample Input 1

8 3
1 1 3 2 2 1 3 1
3 2 3

Sample Output 1

5

Explanation

The valid configuration of platoons for sample 1 are the following:

  • 1 1 3 | 2 | 2 1 3 1
  • 1 1 3 | 2 2 | 1 3 1
  • 1 1 3 | 2 2 1 | 3 1
  • 1 1 3 2 | 2 | 1 3 1
  • 1 1 3 2 | 2 1 | 3 1

There are 5 valid ways of dividing the platoons. There are no possible configurations other than the stated above.

Constraints

  • $1 \le M \le N \le 2000$
  • $1 \le A_i, B_i \le 1\,000\,000\,000$
