Description

In the beginning, Mr. Chanek has a word with $N$ characters. Mr. Chanek who just wants some fun would like to alter a character in the word into some other characters.

For example, Mr. Chanek has the word "SAYA" and Mr. Chanek would like to alter character "A" into "ANG". After the altering, the word becomes "SANGYANG".

Mr. Chanek would like to do this altering operation $M$ time(s). On the $i$-th operation, Mr. Chanek will alter character $A_i$ into word $B_i$. Now Mr. Chanek asks, after $M$ alter(s), what is the length of the word he has

Input Format

The first line consists of two integers $N$ and $M$, representing the length of the word, and the number of altering operations to be done, respectively.

The second line consists of a string expressing the word Mr. Chanek has in the beginning.

The next $M$ line consists of a character and a string separated by a space, expressing the character to be altered, and the word which the character should be altered to, respectively.

Output Format

An integer expressing the length of the word after $M$ altering(s).

Sample Input 

5 3
SENAM
S ER
E AA
A B

Sample Output 

8

Explanation

After the first altering, the word becomes "ERENAM".

After the second altering, the word becomes "AARAANAM".

After the third altering, the word becomes "BBRBBNBM".

Constraints

  • $1 \le N, M \le 100$
  • $|B_i| \ge 1$ for all $1 \le i \le M$
  • The sum of the length of characters on $B_i$ does not exceed $100$.
  • All characters consist of capital letters A-Z.
