Description

Chanek is a country consisting of $N$ cities and connected with $N-1$ bidirectional roads. The $i$-th road connects city $U_i$ and $V_i$ and it costs $C_i$ to pave that road. In the beginning, all roads in Chanek are unpaved. Mr. Chanek would like to pave the roads

Mr. Chanek will make $Q$ trips in succession. On the $i$-th trip, Mr. Chanek will depart from city $A_i$ to city $B_i$. For each trip, Mr. Chanek would like the shortest path from $A_i$ to $B_i$ are all paved. Mr. Chanek would like to know the total cost to pave all unpaved roads on the shortest path from city $A_i$ to city $B_i$. After that, Mr. Chanek will pave all the roads on the shortest path from city $A_i$ to city $B_i$. Paved roads remains paved for future trips.

Help Mr. Chanek!

Input Format

The first line consists of two integers $N$ and $Q$ representing the number of cities and the number of trips, respectively.

The following $N-1$ lines consists of three integers $U_i$, $V_i$, and $C_i$ representing the road connecting city $U_i$ and $V_i$ and price $C_i$ to pave the $i$-th road.

The following $Q$ lines consist of two integers $A_i$ and $B_i$ representing Pak Chanek's trip.

Output Format

Q lines consist of an integer representing the total cost needed for the $i$-th trip.

Sample Input 

5 5
1 2 5
1 3 4
2 4 2
2 5 2
1 5
2 4
2 5
5 3
2 1

Sample Output 

7
2
0
4
0

Constraints

  • $1 \le N, Q \le 200\,000$
  • $1 \le U_i, V_i \le N$ for $1 \le i \le N-1$
  • $1 \le C_i \le 1\,000\,000\,000$ for $1 \le i \le N-1$
  • $1 \le A_i, B_i \le N$ for $1 \le i \le Q$
  • It is guaranteed that every city in Chanek are connected to other cities.
