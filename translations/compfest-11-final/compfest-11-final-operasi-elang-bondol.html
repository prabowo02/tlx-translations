Description

Once upon a time, there was a mighty and brave king named Raja Dengklek. Due to his infinite wisdom, Kerajaan Dengklek became a prosperous nation, rivaling other kingdoms. Raja Dengklek is very proud of his achievement. However, he still wants to accomplish one more achievement, so he will be memorialized for all eternity. In the end, he decided to expand his kingdom. To do so, he will conquer Kerajaan Bondol, the neighboring kingdom.

Kerajaan Bondol consists of $N$ cities numbered from $1$ to $N$. The cities are connected with $M$ one-way roads. City $1$ is the capital city of Kerajaan Bondol. It is guaranteed that every other city that isn't city $1$, there is a route from that city to the capital city (city $1$).

Raja Dengklek knows that to conquer a nation, he needs to take over the capital city and all other cities will submit soon after. Using that strategy, Raja Dengklek dispatched two of his best governors: Pak Chanek and Pak Ganesh. Their mission is one: To lead Raja Dengklek's army into Kerajaan Bondol's capital city.

To complete his mission, Raja Dengklek will also send in $K$ army battalions, numbered from $1$ to $K$. The $i$-th battalion has been deployed into city $A_i$. The battalion deployment is not unique. Thus, there could be two or more battalions that are deployed in the same city. A battalion can move from its current city to another city iff there is a one-way road connecting the two cities.

Pak Chanek and Pak Ganesh will alternatingly give commands to their soldiers, starting from Pak Chanek. Each turn, a governor can command exactly one battalion to move from its current city to another city that is directly connected to its current city. Because Raja Dengklek wants to finish the war as fast as possible, a governor must give a command each turn they get. The war is said to be over if at least one battalion has reached the capital city.

Pak Chanek and Pak Ganesh are both smart and loyal governors. However, both of them thirst to be renowned. Thus, both Pak Chanek and Pak Ganesh want to be the first person to reach the capital city. They will try to make their rival not be the first to send the army into city $1$.

Pak Chanek is pretty sure that he will be able to defeat Pak Ganesh. However, to guarantee his victory, Pak Chanek asks for your help to direct the army such that Pak Chanek will be the first to reach the capital.

You are asked whether Pak Chanek will win this game or not.

Input Format

The standard input will contain these values:

  • In the first line, there are three integers $N$, $M$, and $K$ â€” the number of cities in Kerajaan Bondol, the number of roads in Kerajaan Bondol, and the number of Raja Dengklek's battalions respectively.
  • The next $M$ lines consist of a pair of integers $U_i$ and $V_i$, denoting a one-way road from $U$ to $V$.
  • The last line consists of $K$ space-separated integers: $A_1, A_2, A_3, \dots, A_K$. $A_i$ is the starting position of the $i$-th battalion.

Output Format

Output Chanek if Pak Chanek can successfully win this game. Output Ganesh if Pak Chanek can't possibly win this game.

Sample Input

7 10 3
2 1
3 2
4 3
4 2
5 4
6 4
6 5
7 6
7 5
7 4
4 6 5

Sample Output

Chanek

Constraints

  • $1 \le N, M, K \le 100\,000$
  • $2 \le A_i \le N$, for all $1 \le i \le K$
  • $N - 1 \le M$
  • It is guaranteed for $i > 1$, the battalion in city $i$ can reach city $1$ using one or more roads.
  • It is guaranteed that the roads that connect the cities do not form a cycle.
