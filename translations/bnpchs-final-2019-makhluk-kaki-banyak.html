Description

Feli is a resident of the Beenus kingdom that has $$$N$$$ legs. One day, the Beenus kingdom will hold a lively party. All of the residents of Beenus are invited, including Feli. To respect the party's owner, everyone should wear a polite and decent clothes.

Feli wants to wear a polite and decent clothes that consist of $$$N$$$ pants, $$$N$$$ socks and $$$N$$$ shoes with the following rules:

  • Shoes can be worn on the $$$i$$$-th foot if and only if a pant and a sock have been worn on the $$$i$$$-th foot
  • Feli is able to change the order of wearing clothes according to her liking but she still has to follow the previous rules.

When the party started, Feli suddenly has a question "How many ways are there to wear pants, socks and shoes if she follows the rules above". Help Feli to answer that question. Because the answer can be very large, Feli wants the answer modulo $$$M$$$.

Input Format

Input starts with a positive integer $$$T$$$ denoting the number of cases. T lines after it consist of two positive variables $$$N$$$ and $$$M$$$ where $$$N$$$ denotes the number of Feli's legs and $$$M$$$ denotes the divider.

Output Format

For every case, output "Case #$$$X$$$: $$$Y$$$" where $$$X$$$ denotes the case number starting from 1 and $$$Y$$$ denotes the answer according to the description.

Constraints

  • $$$1 ≤ T ≤ 100$$$.
  • $$$1 ≤ N ≤ 10^{5}$$$.
  • $$$N < M ≤ 2 \times 10^{9}$$$.

Sample Input 1

3
1 5
2 101
2 50

Sample Output 1

Case #1: 2
Case #2: 80
Case #3: 30

Explanation

Some possible ways for case 2 are:
Pant 1 Sock 1 Shoe 1 Pant 2 Sock 2 Shoe 2
Way 1 1 3 6 4 2 5
Way 2 1 5 6 3 2 4
Way 3 1 2 3 4 5 6
The numbers on the table denotes the sequence of wearing.
